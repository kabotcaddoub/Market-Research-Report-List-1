<p><p>The presbyopia treatment device market encompasses various types of interventions designed to improve near vision for individuals affected by age-related vision decline. Corneal inlays are small, implanted devices placed in the cornea to enhance depth of field and allow clear vision at close range. Scleral implants, on the other hand, are placed on the eye's surface and work by altering the eye's optical properties to improve focus. Both options provide alternatives to traditional corrective lenses, catering to diverse patient needs.</p></p>
