<p><p>The gene editing market comprises several key types: Zinc Finger Nucleases (ZFNs), which utilize engineered zinc finger proteins to target specific DNA sequences; Transcription Activator-Like Effector-based Nucleases (TALENs), which combine DNA-binding domains with nucleases for precise gene modifications; and the CRISPR-Cas system, a revolutionary tool that uses RNA guides to locate and edit specific genes, known for its simplicity and efficiency. Each technology offers unique advantages, catering to diverse applications in research, therapeutics, and agriculture.</p></p>
