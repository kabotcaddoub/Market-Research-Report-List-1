<p><p>The tissue forceps market is segmented into two main types: non-locking and locking types. Non-locking forceps are designed for quick grasping and holding of tissues without the need for a locking mechanism, making them ideal for rapid procedures. In contrast, locking forceps feature a mechanism that secures the tissue grasped, allowing for hands-free use during longer or more complex surgeries. Both types play crucial roles in surgical applications, catering to different procedural needs and surgeon preferences.</p></p>
