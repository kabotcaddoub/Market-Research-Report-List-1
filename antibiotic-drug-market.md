<p><p>The antibiotic drug market comprises various classes of antibiotics, each with distinct mechanisms and uses. Penicillins target bacterial cell wall synthesis, while aminoglycosides disrupt protein synthesis. Tetracyclines inhibit bacterial growth by interfering with protein synthesis as well, and chloramphenicol has a broad spectrum but is used cautiously due to toxicity. Macrolides also prevent protein synthesis and are often prescribed for respiratory infections. The "Others" category includes less common antibiotics and novel agents addressing resistant infections, reflecting the evolving landscape of antibiotic therapy.</p></p>
