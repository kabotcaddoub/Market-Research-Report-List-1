<p><p>Output mode cleaners are devices used to enhance the quality of laser beams. The market is divided into two main types: resonant and non-resonant. Resonant type cleaners utilize feedback mechanisms to selectively filter specific wavelengths, ensuring high precision and efficiency in applications requiring tight beam quality. In contrast, non-resonant type cleaners operate on broader spectral ranges, providing greater versatility and ease of use but with potentially lower quality control. Both types cater to distinct needs within the optical and photonics industry.</p></p>
