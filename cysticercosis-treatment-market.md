<p><p>Cysticercosis treatment primarily involves two key antiparasitic medications: Praziquantel and Albendazole. Praziquantel is effective in targeting the larval stage of the Taenia solium parasite, rapidly enhancing the immune response to eliminate cysts. Albendazole, on the other hand, works by disrupting the metabolic functions of the parasites, leading to their death. Both medications can be used in conjunction with supportive therapies, depending on the severity of the infection and the patient's overall health, contributing significantly to the cysticercosis treatment market.</p></p>
